Friction is a resistive force that opposes motion or the tendency of motion between surfaces in contact.

Friction Type Formula Key Characteristics
Static Friction fs≤μsN Prevents motion from starting. Its magnitude matches the applied force up to a maximum value.
Kinetic Friction fk=μkN Acts on a sliding object with a constant magnitude. Typically, μₖ < μₛ.

Drag is the resistive force from a fluid (like air) that opposes an object's motion. When the drag force equals the force of gravity, the object reaches a constant terminal speed.

Condition Drag Force (Magnitude) Terminal Speed Formula
High Speed / Turbulent Flow Fd=12CρAv2 vt=2mgCρA
Low Speed / Laminar Flow Fd=bv vt=mgb

An object in uniform circular motion has a constant speed but a continuously changing velocity, meaning it is always accelerating. This acceleration, directed toward the center of the circle, is caused by a net centripetal force.

Quantity Formula Notes
Centripetal Acceleration ac=v2r Always directed toward the center of the circle.
Centripetal Force ∑Fr=mac=mv2r This is the *net force* toward the center, not a separate force. It is the result of tension, friction, gravity, etc.
Ideal Banked Curve
(No Friction)		 tanθ=v2rg Angle where the normal force's horizontal component provides the full centripetal force.

Example 1: Crate Pulled at an Angle with Friction

A 20 kg crate is pulled by a 100 N force at 30° above the horizontal. If μₖ = 0.2, what is the crate's acceleration?

1. Analyze Vertical Forces (ΣFᵧ = 0):

The upward component of the pull reduces the normal force. N + Fsin(30°) - mg = 0

N = mg - Fsin(30°) = (20)(9.81) - 100(0.5) = 196.2 - 50 = 146.2 N

2. Analyze Horizontal Forces (ΣFₓ = ma):

The net force is the horizontal pull component minus kinetic friction. fₖ = μₖN = 0.2 × 146.2 = 29.24 N

F_net = Fcos(30°) - fₖ = 100(0.866) - 29.24 = 86.6 - 29.24 = 57.36 N

a = F_net / m = 57.36 N / 20 kg = 2.87 m/s²

Answer: The crate accelerates at 2.87 m/s².

Example 2: Maximum Speed on a Flat Curve

What is the max speed a 1500 kg car can navigate a flat curve of radius 50 m if μₛ = 0.9?

1. Identify Centripetal Force: On a flat curve, static friction provides the centripetal force.

F_c = fₛ_max → mv2r=μsN

2. Solve for v: On flat ground, N = mg.

mv2r=μsmg

The mass 'm' cancels out:

v=μsgr=0.9×9.81×50≈21.0 m/s

Answer: The maximum speed is 21.0 m/s.

Core Strategy:

  1. Draw FBD: Isolate the object and draw all external forces acting on it.
  2. Choose Axes: Align one axis with the direction of acceleration.
  3. Apply ΣF = ma: Write component equations for each axis. (e.g., ΣFₓ = ma, ΣFᵧ = 0).
  4. Solve: Solve the resulting system of algebraic equations.

Common Sticking Points:

  • Forgetting N ≠ mg on an incline or with vertical forces.
  • Using kinetic friction (fₖ = μₖN) for a static situation.
  • Treating centripetal force (mv²/r) as a separate force on the FBD. It is the *net result* of other forces.
